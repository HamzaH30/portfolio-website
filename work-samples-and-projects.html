Hamza's Work

Over the past few years, I have worked on numerous projects both, in school and outside of school. The following are a few diverse projects that I've worked on over the last few years.

Best Overall Group Project

In 2018, my friend and I had been working on a science fair project for about 3 months. Our science fair project was called: "Does temperature affect the bounce of a tennis ball?" We had conducted multiple experiments during different conditions to see the result and test our hypothesis. In the Manitoba Schools Science Symposium (MSSS) we had won a gold medal, Best Overall Group Project, and a special award from Engineers Geoscientists Manitoba. This was a very interesting experience for me and helped spark my interest in science and science fairs.

Space Odyssey Essay

In Grade 9, I wrote an essay titled: "How Space Odyssey's HAL shows why we should not be dependent on technology." After watching the movie, 2001: A Space Odyssey, I was supposed to write an essay relating to the movie. I had observed that the sentient computer, HAL, represents a lot of the dark truths of technology today. I had spent a lot of time planning and writing this essay in order to make it as polished it can be. I also included proper MLA formatting. I received full marks for my essay. This essay is a great example of my writing skills and the level of professionalism I include in my essays.

Arcade Machine

In our Software Development class, as a final assessment project, we were assigned into groups and had to build a fully working arcade. We had to build the arcade and build an arcade game to go along with it as well. In our group, we had decided to make a 2D platformer game, with our own characters & sounds. I was responsible for coding the game as well as being the team lead. I used the Unity Game Engine and the programming language, C#, to create the game. Our game is called "Food Fight" and the player has to control a taco character and reach a diamond at the end of each level. However, there are different traps and enemies in the way that the player has to avoid.

The Daily Martian

In grade 8, my friends and I had to make a video, essay, etc. with a dystopian theme. We had decided to make a futuristic newspaper called "The Daily Martian". It was meant to be a newspaper written for a human settlement on Mars. We decided to make a special edition of "The Daily Martian" covering different (made-up) news articles about an artificial intelligence/nuclear war. We decided to have the special edition be made up of five articles that would be connected to one another. Each one of us had to pick which article we wanted to write. Furthermore, I was responsible for the design of the entire newspaper and also added additional features (i.e. "Get to know the author", "Did you know?", etc.). My article is on the last page of the newspaper and it's where I write about the impacts the war had on humanity.

Best in Grade Science Fair

In grade 7, I once again participated in the science fair. This time I decided to do an independent project. My science fair was titled "The Effects of Financial News on Investor Behaviour in the Stock Market". I had worked on my science fair project for more than 4 months! In this science fair project, I improved my research, presentation, and report-writing skills. I used proper terminology in different parts of my report which my judges commended. For my test subjects, I had various people from different age groups and experience in the stock market participate in my experiment. In the Acadia Junior High science fair, I won a gold medal and a Best in Grade award. In the Manitoba Schools Science Symposium (MSSS) I won a silver medal.

The Oily Crossway

In grade 7, one of our final science assignments we needed to do was to build a bridge out of a certain material. Every group was assigned a specific material to make their bridge out of. My group was assigned to use spaghetti to make the bridge. I was assigned as the team leader and was supposed to oversee the project. Our first problem was that we weren't sure what our bridge's design should be. I did a bit of research online and found a specific design that was very strong and rigid. Another problem we faced was that since there were different types of spaghetti we needed to decide what type we should pick. I decided that we should modify our design to use Fettuccine as the bottom part (road) due to its flat edges. Another major issue that we faced was that we needed to paint our bridge, we originally tried painting it with normal paint. However, it wasn't an effective solution, we instead opted to use spray paint.

Silent Movie - Lemonade?

Recently, our class was assigned a project where we had to make a silent movie. In my group, I was meant to be the writer. I had to write and layout all the scenes for the silent movie. In the silent movie, a person who's very tired enters a house where there is free lemonade. However, as they enter the house they find themselves in an illusion. Furthermore, a ghost appears complicating matters, even more, resulting in a chase. As the person escapes the house they find the lemonade they were looking for! As they drink the lemonade all their problems end and the ghost gets defeated! This was a very fun and enjoyable project from which I learned a lot about the role a writer has during a movie.

Static Electricity Project

Recently, my class was assigned an assignment where we had to make a video of us demonstrating static electricity. I decided to make a video of me demonstrating static electricity by rubbing a balloon on my head and creating an effect of 'bending' the water. Rubbing the balloon on my head generates the static charge. Then the charged balloon attracts the water creating the bending effect. This was a very interesting project as I was able to conduct an enjoyable and very interesting experiment.
